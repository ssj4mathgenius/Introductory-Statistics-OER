Course Objectives

After completing this course, you will be able to understand the following.

Chapter 1 Objectives

  • Analyze sample data relative to context and souce
  • Distinguish between discrete and continuous data
  • Understand the difference between a parameter and a statistic
  • Understand the difference between discrete and continuous data
  • Understand a variety of sampling methods
  • See a variety of misueses of statistics
  • Examine four common levels of measurment
  • Understand some of the common misueses of statistics
  • Define the following key terms for statistics:
    • Voluntary response sample
    • Simple Random Sample
    • Discrete Data
    • Continuous Data
    • statistical significance

Chapter 2 Objectives

  • Learn about frequency distributions and terminology related to these tables
  • Practice organizing data into frequency and relative frequency distributions
  • Understand the cumulative frequency distribution
  • Know how to make a histogram from a frequency distribution
  • Identify common distribution shapes such as Right Skew, Left Skew, Normal Distribution, and Uniform Distribution
  • Identify the following graphs:
    • Frequency Polygon
    • Time-Series Graph
    • Pareto Chart
    • Pie Chart
    • Dotplot
    • Stem-and-Leaf Plot
  • Examine and Know what makes a deceptive graph
