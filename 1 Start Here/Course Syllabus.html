General Education Mathematics Learning Outcomes:

Students will demonstrate the ability to:

  • Make meaningful connections between mathematics and other disciplines.
  • Use mathematics to model real world behaviors and apply mathematical concepts to the solution of real-life problems.
  • Apply mathematics to solve problems and determine if the solutions are reasonable.
  • Apply mathematical and/or basic statistical reasoning to analyze date and graphs.
  • Use technology for mathematical reasoning and problem solving.

Minimum Course Outcomes

After completion of this course, students should be able to:

  • Construct and interpret charts and tables of the data.
  • Use binomial and frequency distributions.
  • Recognize mutually exclusive, compound, and independent events and compute their probabilities.
  • Interpret standardized scores and use the normal distribution.
  • Calculate correlation coefficients.
  • Recognize appropriate sampling.
  • Apply the central limit theorem.
  • Use hypothesis testing and levels of confidence.

Grading

So, as a recap of the above, your grade will be calculated as follows:

  • Homework 30%
  • Participation/Weekly Checks/Projects 5%
  • Quizzes 10%
  • 3 Test 30% total (10% each)
  • Final Exam 25%

Grades will be assigned according to the following scale:

  • 90% to 100% = a grade of A
  • 80% to 89% = a grade of B
  • 70% to 79% = a grade of C
  • 65% to 69% = a grade of D
  • Anything Below 65% = a grade of F

A grade of FA will be used to designate an unofficial withdrawal for those students who stop working in the class or do not complete the Final Exam. Grades are not mailed to students. Grades may be accessed through Web for Students five (5) working days after grades are required in Admissions.
