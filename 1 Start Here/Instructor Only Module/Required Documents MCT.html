What To Do About Inaccessible Items

When informational materials and technologies do not conform to THEC/TBR accessibility standards, it is up to the course developer to create an Equally Effective Alternate Access Plan (Word Doc will download).

An Equally Effective Alternate Access Plan (EEAAP) is a valuable resource that an institution can use if it is necessary to buy, develop, or use a technology that is not fully accessible. This plan describes how to provide alternate access to the same information or services offered by less-than-accessible technology. Having an EEAAP in place for technologies that are not fully accessible will help ensure that all students will have fair and equal access in a timely manner as required by law.

The EEAAP should ensure that the learning objectives presented in the original material are covered in an alternate way. Students shouldn't miss out on content or assessments that help to meet the course learning objectives.
