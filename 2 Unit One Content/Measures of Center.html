Measures of Center

Statistics provides tools to understand and summarize data. Measures of center are essential in statistics because they give us a single value that represents the entire dataset. This helps in making decisions, identifying trends, and comparing datasets effectively. The three most commonly used measures of center are the mean, median, and mode.

Mean

Definition: Mean

The mean, also commonly known as the "average", is the sum of all data values divided by the number of values. The sample mean is often denoted as \( \bar{x} \), while the population mean is denoted as \( \mu \). Sometimes the mathematical formulas for calculating the mean are given as shown below:

\[ \text{Sample Mean: } \bar{x} = \frac{\sum x}{n} \]

There are several times throughout the course you may encounter formulas used to find a particular statistic so we will attempt to explain some of the symbols as we progress. The \(\sum \) symbol in mathematics means "add", the \( x \) symbol represents data values in the sample, and \( n \) represents the number of values in the sample. So interpreting the formula above for finding the sample mean \( \bar{x} \) is found by adding all the data values and dividing by the number of values in the dataset.

While there are formulas for many of the statistics used to describe datasets, it is important to not focus so much on the formula but to understand the meaning of the values. When trying to understand the mean, it is important to keep these key concepts in mind:

  • Sample means drawn from the same population tend to vary less than other measures of center
  • The mean takes every data value into consideration
  • If there are extreme values (outliers) in the dataset, they can impact the mean substantially

Example : Calculating the Mean

Consider the following data representing test scores: 75, 80, 85, 90, 95.

In order to find the mean for this sample of test scores, we will just sum up all the scores and then divide by the number of scores. This gives us the following \[ \bar{x} = \frac{75 + 80 + 85 + 90 + 95}{5} = 85 \]

Median

The median can be described as the "middle value" of a dataset, where approximately half of the values are smaller than the median and the other half are larger. A more precise definition follows.

Definition: Median

The median is the middle value in an ordered dataset. If the dataset has an odd number of values, the median is the center value. If the dataset has an even number of values, the median is the average of the two center values.

When interpreting the median it is important to keep a few important properties in mind:

  • The median is not effected by extreme values in the dataset
  • The median does not take every data value into consideration

Mode

Definition: Mode

The mode is the most frequent value in a dataset. A dataset can have one mode (unimodal), more than one mode (multimodal), or no mode.

It is important to note that the mode isn't used as much with quantitative dat, but it is the only measure of center applicable to qualitative (or categorical) data.

Example : Determining the Mode

Consider the dataset: red, blue, green, blue, blue, red.

Solution

The mode is blue, as it appears most frequently.

Each measure of center provides unique insights into a dataset, making them critical tools for data analysis. Measures of center can always be calculated from a sample, but it is crucial to consider if doing so is meaningful. For nominal data, which consist of names or categories, calculations like the mean or median are meaningless. Additionally, the validity of these statistics depends on the soundness of the sampling method; poor methods can lead to misleading results.
