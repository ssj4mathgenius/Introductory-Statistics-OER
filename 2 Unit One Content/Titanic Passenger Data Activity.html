Titanic Passenger Data Activity

Using Graphs to Summarize a Data Set

  1. Each member of your group was given a set of data from a random sample of 35 passengers from the Titanic disaster. Discuss the term “random sample.” How was this sample likely obtained to give to you?
  2. Discuss the different data columns. What type of data is in each column? (e.g. qualitative, discrete quantitative, or continuous quantitative)
  3. You may have blank values under the Age column. Why do you think that may have happened? You may have decimal values in the Age column. What does that mean?
  4. In previous classes, you have probably learned how to create graphs to summarize a data set. Plan together with your group how to summarize your data set with a graph or table in a way your group agrees on and explain in the space below why your choice to summarize the data in this way will be beneficial.
  5. Summarize your own data set using the plan you made with your group. (Table, graph, etc.)
  6. Compare your data summary to those of your class as a whole (the instructor will initiate this). List at least one similarity and one difference.
  7. How would your summary change if you were asked the question “Were the women on the Titanic more likely to survive than the men?”
